When the U.S. Open begins on Monday, women's tennis star Naomi Osaka will draw the world's attention — both for how she'll play and what she might say. The tour's third-ranked player wore COVID-19 masks at last year's tournament that featured the names of Black victims of police violence, and she quit the French Open in May with an explanation on Instagram about her mental health struggles that has garnered more than 865,000 likes.Osaka can count on support from one of the sport's legends, Billie Jean King, who in a recent interview with Yahoo Finance praised outspoken young athletes like Osaka and gymnast Simon Biles.
